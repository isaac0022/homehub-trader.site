What is Binary Options Trading?

Binary options trading is one of the simplest forms of financial trading. The word "binary" means two outcomes — you either win or lose. You predict whether the price of an asset will go UP or DOWN within a specific time period.

If your prediction is correct, you win a fixed payout. If wrong, you lose your stake. That's it — simple and straightforward.

💡 Example: You predict Volatility 75 Index will RISE in the next 5 minutes. You stake $1. If you're right — you win $0.85 profit. If wrong — you lose your $1 stake.

Digits Trading: Even/Odd, Over/Under & Matches Strategy

May 2025 · 7 min read · By HomeHub Trader

What is Digits Trading?

Digits trading is one of the most popular trade types on Deriv, especially among Kenyan traders. Instead of predicting price direction, you predict the LAST DIGIT of the price at expiry.

For example, if the price is 1234.567 — the last digit is 7. Digits trading is all about predicting what that last digit will be.

Types of Digits Trades

🔢 Even/Odd: Predict whether the last digit will be even (0,2,4,6,8) or odd (1,3,5,7,9). Payout is around 95% making it one of the best value trades on Deriv.
⬆️⬇️ Over/Under: Predict whether the last digit will be over 4 (5,6,7,8,9) or under 5 (0,1,2,3,4). Similar payout to Even/Odd.
🎯 Matches/Differs: Predict the exact last digit (0-9). Matches pays around 800% but is harder to win. Differs pays around 5% but wins 90% of the time.

Even/Odd Strategy

The key to Even/Odd trading is using statistical analysis. Use our HomeHub Trader Digits Analysis Tool to check the current Even/Odd ratio before trading:

  • ✅ If Even is above 55% — consider trading ODD (it's due)
  • ✅ If Odd is above 55% — consider trading EVEN (it's due)
  • ⏳ If ratio is close to 50/50 — wait for a clearer signal

Over/Under Strategy

  • ✅ Check the Over/Under ratio on our analysis tool

How to Manage Your Money Trading Binary Options

May 2025 · 4 min read · By HomeHub Trader

Why Money Management is Everything

Most traders don't lose because of bad strategies — they lose because of bad money management. You can have the best strategy in the world but if you risk too much per trade you will eventually blow your account.

The 2% Rule

💡 Never risk more than 2% of your total account balance on a single trade. If you have $100 — maximum stake per trade is $2. If you have $50 — maximum stake is $1.

Why the 2% Rule Works

Even if you lose 10 trades in a row (which happens to everyone), you still have 80% of your account left. This gives you time to recover without going broke.

Set a Daily Loss Limit

  • ✅ Decide your maximum daily loss before you start
  • ✅ If you hit that limit — STOP trading for the day
  • ✅ Never chase losses — emotional trading destroys accounts
  • ✅ A good daily loss limit is 10% of your account

Set a Daily Profit Target

  • ✅ Set a realistic daily profit target — 5-10% of account
  • ✅ Once you hit your target — STOP trading
  • ✅ Greedy traders always give back their profits

The Golden Rules

  • 🔴 Never trade scared money — only trade what you can afford to lose
  • 🔴 Never borrow money to trade
  • 🔴 Never trade when emotional — angry, sad, or desperate
  • 🔴 Never try to recover losses in one trade
  • 🟢 Always use a demo account first
  • 🟢 Keep a trading journal
  • 🟢 Review your trades every week
💪 Remember: Consistent small profits beat big risky wins every time. Slow and steady wins the trading race.
